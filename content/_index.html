57+ Blocks Coalition
A Gun Violence Reduction Strategy

A Coalition of Blocks. Organizing Citywide for Change.
Building Safe, Resource-rich, and Healthy Communities to Live.

Overview

In 2021, the Philadelphia Inquirer published "Intersections of Injustice," an article that identified 57 blocks where 10 or more people have been shot since 2015. Inspired by this work, the Philadelphia District Attorney's Office (DAO) and community partners launched the 57+ Blocks Coalition, a group that supports place-based strategies to reduce gun violence through the targeted investment of resources, services, and environmental improvements.

Vision

Grounded in the reality that we are more powerful united than we are divided, we leverage collaboration between community and faith-based organizations, government agencies, community leaders, youth, and others to address historical disinvestment and related harms that sustain cycles of violence and trauma. Our coalition actively engages communities across Philadelphia to bring resources to the people and places that need them most.
